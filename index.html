Silliest Cars

Smart Crossblade

Year: 2003

It's the infamous Smart Car but with no roof. Not like a convertible. There is no roof whatsoever. Or doors. And it's beautiful.

Subaru XT

Year: 1985

This is the pinnacle of wacky design experimentation from the 80s. The interior was inspired by fighter jet cockpits and the wheels have squares on them. Like Minecraft.

Hyundai Ioniq 5 N

Year: 2024

In a world where people are mourning the death of the sporty hatchback, Hyundai has brought it back by pretending that their electric crossover is one of them. And at least in my opinion, it's kind of working. It also has fake gear shifting that simulates a gas car which is really neat.

Isuzu VehiCROSS

Year: 1997

Adding some offroad tires to a regular SUV and calling it an offroader is common today, but this is where it all started. Just look at the ridiculous amounts of black plastic. It's so camp.

Mazda MX-30

Year: 2022

Like most compliance cars, vehicles produced solely to meet California regulations, this thing is kind of pathetic. However, years after Mazda stopped putting rotary engines in sports cars, it's surreal to see it make its comeback solely as a range-extending generator for this electric...thing.

Renault Avantime

Year: 2001

Adding a Renault to this list is like cheating because the French automakers are truly living by no rules and doing whatever their hearts desire. One example, of many, is this minivan that has only two doors and a rear that is shaped like some sort of sofa.
